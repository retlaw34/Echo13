30 June 2022

tmtmtl30, tetrazeta updated:

  • Pouring -- you can now pour out a portion of a beaker's contents by clicking on an object with a beaker while on help intent.
  • Liquid cement, made from heated carbon, hydrogen, oxygen, and water; it can be concentrated into hexement by adding phenol. Don't eat it.
  • Concrete, made by adding sand to liquid cement. Similarly, hexacrete can be made by adding sand to a beaker of hexement.
  • Concrete floors, made by pouring concrete or hexacrete onto catwalks over plating. Use a chisel to change how they look, but be careful not to step on them while they're wet.
  • Concrete walls, made by pouring concrete onto metal grilles. They take 30 seconds to fully harden, and will break if subjected to enough punishment.
  • Hexacrete walls, stronger concrete walls that take longer to harden, made by pouring hexacrete onto metal girders. Good for building explosion-resistant bunkers.
